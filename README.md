# Electric-Excursion
Exploring possibility and viability of converting Ford Excursion to EV powered by gas generator (similar to Edison Trucks, but for big-size suvs)

Brainstorm with Gemini:
# Project: Ford Excursion "EV-X" Modernization
**Goal:** Convert a Ford Excursion to a high-performance, lightweight AWD EV with a range-extending generator.

## 1. Powertrain Configuration
* **Drive System:** Dual-Motor All-Wheel Drive (AWD).
* **Front Motor:** Ford F-150 Lightning Front Drive Unit (FDU).
* **Rear Motor:** Ford F-150 Lightning Rear Drive Unit (RDU) or Dana eS9000r.
* **Range Extender:** Horse C15 Turbo Generator (120kW / 161hp).
* **Battery:** 100kWh High-Voltage Pack (Tesla or custom cell-to-pack).

## 2. Chassis & Suspension
* **Suspension:** Kelderman 4-Link Air Suspension (Front & Rear).
    * *Feature:* Active highway lowering for aerodynamics.
* **Hubs/Axles:** 6-Lug Ford F-150 Lightning Hub Conversion.
    * *Benefit:* Sheds ~400 lbs of unsprung weight.
* **Wheels:** 20" Lightning Aero-optimized wheels.

## 3. Electronic Control Systems
* **Brain (VCU):** AEM EV VCU300 (Manages dual motors, battery, and generator).
* **Braking:** Bosch i-Booster (Vacuum-less electronic braking + Regen).
* **Steering:** Electric Power Steering (EPS) rack swap.
* **Cooling:** Mishimoto Triple-Pass Aluminum Radiator (Multi-loop system).

## 4. Key Performance Specs (Estimated)
* **Continuous Cruising:** 75 mph indefinitely (Generator-only mode).
* **Total Torque:** ~775 lb-ft (Instant).
* **Weight Reduction:** ~400 lbs lighter than stock V10 (even with 100kWh battery).
* **Cargo:** Full "Mega Frunk" storage in the former engine bay.

So far looks too optimistic, but I want to explore how far can we go with LLMs research and exploration.
